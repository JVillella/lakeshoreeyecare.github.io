Dr. Ivy Koh

Dr. Ivy Koh obtained a Bachelor of Science degree from the University of Waterloo in 2002, graduating with honours. She further completed her Doctorate of Optometry at the University of Waterloo in 2006, earning the A.W. Cole Award for Clinical Excellence and the E.J Chisholm Memorial Scholarship for Pediatrics.

Dr. Koh completed clinical training in ocular disease and therapeutics at the prestigious Omni Eye Services in Atlanta, Georgia, working with ophthalmologists specializing in glaucoma, corneal and retinal disease. Her interest in ocular disease led to further work as a co-managing optometrist at the ophthalmology centre EyeMD Institute in Brampton, Ontario.

She has clinical interests in pediatrics, specialty contact lens fittings (toric, multifocal, rigid and ortho-keratology), as well as lasik surgery co-management, having worked at a large refractive surgery centre for several years.

Dr. Koh had the unique experience of volunteering as an optometrist during the 2010 Winter Olympic and Para-Olympic games in Vancouver, BC providing eye examinations for athletes, coaches and trainers from around the globe.

Born and raised in Mississauga, Dr. Koh is passionate about providing quality family eye care for the community. She is a proud member of the Ontario Association of Optometrists, Canadian Association of Optometrists and the Canadian Coalition for Eye Care Professionals.
